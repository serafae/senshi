Michiru Kaiou (Sailor Neptune) ♆'s Interests

General

I live close to the sea whenever I can. Its rhythm steadies me — calm and power moving together, never in conflict.


I love painting, traveling, and finding quiet places where the world feels unhurried.


My life is a balance between instinct and refinement; I let neither rule the other...

Music

Classical music defines me. I often play Debussy, Saint-Saëns, and Bach, but I’m drawn to any piece that carries emotion beneath control.


I practice every day — not for perfection, but to stay honest with myself.


A violin can soothe or command; I prefer when it does both.

Movies

I don’t watch often, but when I do, I look for films that move like music — quiet, deliberate, and full of feeling between the lines.


I appreciate stories that value atmosphere over spectacle.


A simple film with sincerity speaks to me more than one that shouts its meaning.

Television

I enjoy performances — ballet, orchestral concerts, and documentaries about the ocean or the arts.


There’s beauty in watching others devote themselves fully to their craft.


Haruka says I have “serious taste,” but I think of it as curiosity with purpose.

Books

Poetry and philosophy remind me to look inward.


I read Rilke for truth, Soseki for stillness, and classical Japanese tales for their quiet grace. The Tale of Genji remains a favorite; its beauty lies in what it never says outright.


Reading keeps my mind anchored, even when the tides pull hard.

Heroes

Haruka Tenoh stands beside me — my equal, my challenge, my balance.


Her conviction sharpens mine. Beyond her, I respect anyone who protects others without seeking reward.


True strength doesn’t shout; it endures, steady as the tide.

Michiru Kaiou (Sailor Neptune) ♆'s Blurbs

About me:

I am Michiru Kaiou — artist, violinist, and someone who refuses to drift with the current.


I move with intention, and I never mistake gentleness for weakness. People often compare me to the sea, but they forget how deep the ocean runs, and how easily it can shift from calm to storm.


I live for elegance, precision, and the beauty that hides in discipline. Whether I’m on stage or facing a challenge, I meet it head-on. Perfection isn’t something I chase — it’s something I create, moment by moment. The ocean teaches that strength and grace belong together, and I’ve never doubted that truth.


Those who know me understand that I don’t need to announce what I’m capable of. I simply am. Still, if you listen closely, you might catch the sound of the waves in every note I play — and maybe, if you believe in destiny, you’ll recognize the power that stirs beneath the surface.
